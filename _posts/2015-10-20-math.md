It holds `$$ |\overline{\mathbf{L}}| = \sum_{\mathcal{T} \in \text{ST}(\overline{\W})} \prod_{ij} \mathcal{T}_{ij} $$`.

where `$$\text{ST}(\overline{\W})$$` is the set of spanning trees of `$$\overline{\W}$$`.

Relying on the above matrix tree theorem for weighted graphs, one can easily compute the determinant of graph Laplacians on $\mathcal{S}_W$ as follows.

For $\W \in \mathcal{S}_W$, $|\overline{\bm{L}}| = \prod_{r=1}^R |\gamma(r)|$ where $\gamma(r)$ is the set of edges in the unique cycle associated to the $r^{th}$ connected component of $\W$.

One can notice that a connected component of $\overline{\W}$ is a spanning tree with one cycle (CRSF). In the below derivation, equation \ref{cycle_trick} is derived by noticing that to obtain a spanning tree from a CRSF, one only has to remove an edge from the cycle.
\begin{align*}
    |\overline{\bm{L}}| &= \sum_{\mathcal{T} \in \text{ST}(\Tilde{\W})} \prod_{ij} \mathcal{T}_{ij} \\
    &= \prod_{ij} \overline{W}_{ij} \prod_{r=1}^R \sum_{i' j' \in \gamma(r)} \overline{W}_{i'j'}^{-1} \label{cycle_trick}\\
    &= \prod_{r=1}^R |\gamma(r)| \quad \text{for unitary graph } \W \in \mathcal{S}_{\scaleto{W}{4pt}} \:.
\end{align*}
